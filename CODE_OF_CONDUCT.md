# Code of Conduct

SkillRun aims to be a focused, high-trust engineering community.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and direct.
- Assume good intent, then verify with evidence.
- Critique ideas, designs, and patches rather than people.
- Keep discussions relevant to SkillRun's product goals, runtime safety, and user experience.
- Make space for maintainers and contributors to ask basic questions without embarrassment.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, or personal attacks.
- Discriminatory language or exclusionary behavior.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Repeatedly derailing technical discussions after maintainers ask to refocus.
- Abusive, spammy, or bad-faith contributions.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments; close issues or pull requests; block users; or take other reasonable action to protect the project. Enforcement should be proportionate, documented when appropriate, and focused on keeping the project safe and productive.
